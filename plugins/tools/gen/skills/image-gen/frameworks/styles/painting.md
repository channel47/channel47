# Painting: Artistic Expression

From Renaissance oils to impressionist landscapes to contemporary abstracts, painting styles carry centuries of artistic tradition.

## Core Principles

### 1. Brushwork is Voice

The way paint is applied carries as much meaning as the subject.

**In Practice:**
- Impasto: thick, textured strokes for energy
- Smooth blending: classical, refined technique
- Visible brushwork: impressionistic, expressive
- Dry brush: texture and atmosphere

**Prompt Keywords:**
- visible brushstrokes, impasto technique
- smooth blending, sfumato
- loose brushwork, expressive strokes
- palette knife, thick paint
- watercolor washes, ink wash

### 2. Color Theory in Practice

Painters think about color relationships, not just individual hues.

**In Practice:**
- Warm/cool contrast creates depth
- Complementary colors vibrate
- Limited palettes create harmony
- Color temperature shifts in shadows

**Prompt Keywords:**
- warm palette, cool tones
- complementary colors, analogous colors
- limited palette, earth tones
- vibrant, saturated, muted
- chiaroscuro, tenebrism

### 3. Medium Matters

Each painting medium has distinct characteristics.

**In Practice:**
- Oil: rich, blendable, traditional
- Watercolor: transparent, flowing, delicate
- Acrylic: versatile, bold, modern
- Gouache: opaque, flat, illustrative

**Prompt Keywords:**
- oil painting, oil on canvas
- watercolor, aquarelle
- acrylic painting, gouache
- fresco, tempera
- digital painting, Procreate style

### 4. Artistic Movements

Reference historical movements for specific aesthetics.

**In Practice:**
- Impressionism: light, color, moment
- Expressionism: emotion, distortion, intensity
- Realism: accurate, detailed, observational
- Abstract: non-representational, pure form

**Prompt Keywords:**
- impressionist style, Monet-like
- expressionist, Van Gogh style
- classical realism, academic painting
- abstract expressionist, contemporary art
- baroque, renaissance, romantic period

### 5. Composition and Light

Painters manipulate reality for dramatic effect.

**In Practice:**
- Dramatic lighting (chiaroscuro) for impact
- Atmospheric perspective for depth
- Rule of thirds or golden ratio
- Strategic focal points

**Prompt Keywords:**
- dramatic lighting, chiaroscuro
- atmospheric perspective, sfumato
- golden ratio composition
- focal point, leading the eye
- vignette, spotlight effect

## When to Use This Style

- Fine art and gallery pieces
- Emotional and expressive imagery
- Book covers and editorial art
- Wall art and prints
- Concept art with traditional feel
- Portraits with artistic flair
- Landscapes and nature scenes

- **+ Photography:** Painterly photo effects, digital painting from reference
- **+ Illustration:** Illustrated with painterly textures
