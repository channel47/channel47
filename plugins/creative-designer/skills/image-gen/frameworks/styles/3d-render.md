# 3D Render: Dimensional Reality

## The Essence

3D rendering creates images from constructed virtual worlds. It offers control impossible in photography—perfect materials, impossible angles, physics-defying scenes. The aesthetic ranges from photorealistic product shots to stylized abstract art. The key is thinking dimensionally: light interacts with surfaces in predictable, manipulable ways.

## Core Principles

### 1. Materials Define Everything

Surface properties create visual interest and realism.

**In Practice:**
- Specify material properties: matte, glossy, metallic, transparent
- Consider subsurface scattering for skin, wax, marble
- Roughness affects reflections
- Combine materials for complex objects

**Prompt Keywords:**
- glossy, matte, metallic, chrome
- glass, transparent, translucent
- brushed metal, polished, reflective
- plastic, rubber, ceramic, fabric
- subsurface scattering, iridescent

### 2. Lighting Setups

3D lighting follows studio photography principles but with total control.

**In Practice:**
- Three-point lighting for product shots
- HDRI environments for realistic reflections
- Rim lights separate subject from background
- Colored lights for mood

**Prompt Keywords:**
- studio lighting, three-point lighting
- HDRI lighting, environment lighting
- soft box, rim light, backlight
- neon lighting, colored lights
- global illumination, ambient occlusion

### 3. Camera and Perspective

Virtual cameras can do anything real cameras can—and more.

**In Practice:**
- Isometric for clarity and cuteness
- Low angle for power and drama
- Macro for detail and texture
- Impossible angles for impact

**Prompt Keywords:**
- isometric view, orthographic
- low angle, high angle, bird's eye
- macro, close-up, wide shot
- floating, suspended, impossible perspective
- depth of field, tilt-shift effect

### 4. Render Quality

The level of realism in the final output.

**In Practice:**
- Path tracing for photorealism
- Stylized rendering for artistic effect
- Clean renders vs. artistic noise
- Ray tracing for accurate reflections

**Prompt Keywords:**
- ray tracing, path tracing
- octane render, cinema 4d, blender
- photorealistic render, hyperrealistic
- stylized 3D, low poly
- clean render, studio render

### 5. Composition and Scene

Building the virtual world around your subject.

**In Practice:**
- Minimal backgrounds for product focus
- Full environments for context
- Abstract geometric backgrounds
- Infinite curves and gradients

**Prompt Keywords:**
- studio background, seamless backdrop
- floating in space, abstract environment
- geometric shapes, curves, spheres
- gradient background, infinite curve
- product shot, packshot

## Recommended Settings

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Model Tier | Pro | Better material handling and detail |
| Best Aspect Ratios | 1:1, 16:9, 4:3 | Product: square; Hero: wide |
| Grounding | Usually false | Constructed scenes don't need it |

## When to Use This Style

- Product visualization and e-commerce
- Tech and gadget presentations
- Abstract art and backgrounds
- Architectural visualization
- Game and entertainment assets
- Brand and identity materials
- Packaging mockups

## Combining With Other Styles

- **+ Photography:** Photorealistic CGI indistinguishable from photos
- **+ Illustration:** Low-poly or flat-shaded 3D illustration
- **+ Painting:** Painterly 3D with artistic post-processing
