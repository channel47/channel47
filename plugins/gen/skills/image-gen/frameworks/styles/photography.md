# Photography: Capturing Reality

## The Essence

Great photographic prompts think like a photographer: consider your lens, your light, and your moment.

## Core Principles

### 1. Lighting is Everything

The quality and direction of light defines the mood entirely.

**In Practice:**
- Specify light source: natural, studio, ambient
- Describe quality: soft, harsh, diffused, dramatic
- Indicate direction: backlit, side-lit, overhead, golden hour

**Prompt Keywords:**
- golden hour, blue hour, soft natural light
- studio lighting, three-point lighting, Rembrandt lighting
- backlit, rim light, soft box, diffused light
- dramatic shadows, high key, low key

### 2. Depth and Focus

Cameras create depth through focus. Use this to direct attention.

**In Practice:**
- Shallow depth of field (bokeh) isolates subjects
- Deep focus keeps everything sharp (landscapes)
- Selective focus guides the viewer's eye

**Prompt Keywords:**
- shallow depth of field, bokeh, blurred background
- 85mm portrait lens, f/1.4, f/2.8
- tack sharp, deep focus, everything in focus
- macro, close-up, telephoto compression

### 3. Lens Character

Different focal lengths create different feels.

**In Practice:**
- Wide (24mm): Environmental, expansive, slight distortion
- Standard (50mm): Natural perspective, documentary
- Portrait (85mm): Flattering compression, subject isolation
- Telephoto (200mm+): Compression, distant subjects brought close

**Prompt Keywords:**
- 35mm lens, 50mm lens, 85mm portrait lens
- wide angle, telephoto, macro lens
- fisheye, tilt-shift, anamorphic

### 4. Composition Fundamentals

Photographic composition follows established rules.

**In Practice:**
- Rule of thirds for balanced framing
- Leading lines draw the eye
- Negative space creates breathing room
- Symmetry for impact

**Prompt Keywords:**
- rule of thirds, centered composition
- leading lines, negative space
- symmetrical, asymmetrical balance
- framed by, foreground interest

### 5. Color and Grading

Post-processing defines the final mood.

**In Practice:**
- Warm tones for comfort, nostalgia
- Cool tones for modern, clinical
- High contrast for drama
- Muted colors for film look

**Prompt Keywords:**
- warm color grading, cool tones
- high contrast, muted colors
- film grain, Kodak Portra, Fuji colors
- desaturated, vibrant, cinematic color grade

## Recommended Settings

| Setting | Value | Reason |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Model Tier | Pro | Better photorealism and detail |
| Best Aspect Ratios | 3:2, 4:3, 16:9 | Standard camera formats |
| Grounding | True for real locations | Improves accuracy of known places |

## When to Use This Style

- Product photography for e-commerce
- Professional headshots and portraits
- Real estate and architectural shots
- Food photography
- Lifestyle and editorial content
- Any time authenticity matters
